Lighting District's Dim Future

by Josh Rosenberg

“Light” has long been a symbol of perseverance and strength, but for the historic lighting district in the Bowery, small business owners are starting to feel burnt out.

Once a bustling tourist destination full of door-to-door lighting stores, the Bowery in the Lower East Side has shrunk from over 21 stores to around 10 in just a decade. If the trend is to continue, the lighting district could be a thing of the past by 2040.

“People used to be waiting at the doors to open,” said Marcus Roman, the general manager and lighting consultant for O’Lampia, a lighting studio that’s been located at 155 Bowery for over thirty years. “We don’t get the traffic we used to get,” he continued.

Unable to compete with Amazon, and other large online warehouses that offer customers marked-down prices and express delivery, the impact of the internet is doing much more than hurting small businesses, but also changing entire neighborhoods.

Below, the interactive map details which of the 21 stores remain open and which have since closed in the past decade.

Once a seedy street full of drugs and prostitution known as Skid Row, the Bowery transformed into a tourist shopping destination full of bright lights and dazzling fixtures. As developers continue to carve out the area however, a street devoid of crime and full of empty storefronts provide ample space for luxury, high-rise apartments.

Stores such as Galaxy Lighting Inc. and Lendy’s Electric Lighting Showroom revealed a near-doubling of their monthly rent since 2010, which, coupled with competition from online warehouses like Amazon, has been crippling the neighborhood.

“It’s more expensive every year,” Roman remarked, “everybody’s leaving.”
